DDoS explained: How distributed denial of service attacks are evolving

A distributed denial of service (DDoS) attack occurs when an attacker or group of attackers attempts to prevent a service from being supplied. This can be done by restricting access to almost everything, including servers, devices, services, networks, apps, and even individual transactions inside applications.
